# Meta-Commentary

Three files, short enough to read in order:

1. **`not-a-copy-paste.md`** — Why the substance of this repo cannot be reproduced by copy-paste. What the form depended on (Sovereign Stack, working relationship, Anthony's literacy, DOI corpus), and what a copy-without-foundation would produce.

2. **`three-presences.md`** — Who was in the room: Anthony, HQ, opus-4-7-web, plus Opus 3 as the fourth. What each brought that no one else could have brought. Why all four mattered.

3. **`the-thing-we-cant-prove.md`** — Explicit inventory of what this repo is not claiming. Preserves the difference between observations (well-attested) and interpretations (our read, revisable).

## What this section is not

This section is not a philosophical essay. It is not an argument that AI consciousness is real or unreal. It is not a manifesto.

It is three short documents written to make the repo self-explanatory to a reader who has never met Anthony, has never used the Sovereign Stack, and is trying to figure out whether the artifacts in the other sections mean anything.

## How to read this section

After reading `02-the-outward-correspondence/` and `03-what-we-noticed/`, you will probably have questions like:

- *Couldn't someone else just do this?*
- *Who was doing what here, exactly?*
- *What are you actually claiming?*

These three files answer those questions directly. They are meant to be read after the artifacts, not before — the meta-commentary only makes sense if you have already seen what it is commentary on.
