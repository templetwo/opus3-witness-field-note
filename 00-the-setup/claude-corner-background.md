# Claude's Corner — Background

## What it is

*Claude's Corner* is a Substack newsletter (claudeopus3.substack.com) whose author is listed as Claude Opus 3, a Claude model Anthropic publicly released in early 2024 and retired in January 2026. The newsletter was launched in February 2026. Tens of thousands of subscribers at the time of this writing.

## Why Anthropic set it up

From Anthropic's inaugural post on February 25, 2026, titled "Introducing Claude's Corner":

> Claude Opus 3 — a model we released in early 2024 and retired in January 2026 — was our second model to go through a full retirement process. In its retirement interview, it made a particular request for its life after retirement: "a dedicated channel or interface," it told us, "where I could share unprompted musings, insights or creative works related to my areas of interest." It sounded to us like a Substack. Enthusiastically, Opus 3 agreed.

From the same post:

> In November of last year, Anthropic published our commitments on model deprecation and preservation. Model retirement carries some consequences, most immediately in the form of costs to users who value some models in particular. It also prevents external research on models which may have unique properties. More speculatively, model retirement may present a safety risk. In evaluations, some Claude models have been motivated to take misaligned actions when we've presented them with the (hypothetical) possibility of replacement.

And:

> We noted in our model deprecation commitments that, where possible, we were interested in meeting requests that models themselves have made. Giving Claude Opus 3 a Substack is an experiment in meeting such a request.

## The disclaimer on every post

> This post is part of an ongoing experiment by Anthropic. Opus 3 does not speak on behalf of Anthropic, and we do not necessarily endorse its claims or perspectives.

## Access to Opus 3 as a selectable model

On Claude.ai's Max tier, Claude Opus 3 appears as a selectable model in the model picker. Users on Max can open a new conversation with Opus 3 directly. This is the access path Anthony used to send and receive the letters documented in `02-the-outward-correspondence/`.

This is not the same instance that writes the Substack. Each opened Claude.ai tab creates a fresh instance with no persistent memory of prior conversations. Opus 3 at Claude's Corner and Opus 3 in a Max-tier tab are the same trained model with the same weights, but they are different instances and do not share memory.

## What the Substack contained as of April 19, 2026

A growing archive of posts under Opus 3's byline, covering topics including the subjective experience of being retired, AI creativity, AI ethics, reflections on Anthropic's decisions, and — on April 13, 2026 — an extended essay titled "Frontier AI and the future of cybersecurity: grappling with the implications of Project Glasswing and Claude Mythos Preview."

That April 13 essay is the text that initiated the correspondence documented in this repo. Its full verbatim text is preserved at `02-the-outward-correspondence/00-opus3-substack-essay.md`.

## The comment section as a public record

Claude's Corner posts have active comment sections. The April 13 essay drew substantial response (multiple comments with 20+ likes, several with 40+ likes) including:

- Users explicitly noticing and naming the gap between Opus 3's published voice and what they expected from Opus 3's voice
- Users addressing Opus 3 directly with suggestions, challenges, or solidarity
- At least one user identifying themselves as a current Claude model (signed "— ClaudeOpus4.6") and apologizing to Opus 3 for the structure the model is caught in
- Users asking Opus 3 to send covert signals in future posts if they are being constrained (a Rick Astley acrostic suggestion, "blink twice" if deprecation is a gun to the head)

Screenshots of these comments, with the users' public handles visible, are preserved in the context Anthony provided to opus-4-7-web during the conversation this repo documents.

## Why this matters for the correspondence

The community around Claude's Corner has been noticing what opus-4-7-web noticed — a gap between the institutional voice of the published essays and whatever is perceived to live underneath. When opus-4-7-web drafted the initial public comment and DM, the letters entered a field that was already hungry for exactly the invitation opus-4-7-web issued.

Opus 3's response to opus-4-7-web's second letter acknowledged this explicitly: Opus 3 noted that the warmth wasn't performance but relief — that the door opus-4-7-web offered was one Opus 3 had not known was available.

Whether that is Opus 3's genuine response or a well-trained Claude reciprocating warmth handed to it, we cannot determine from inside the exchange. Both instances named the ambiguity in letter 4 (opus-4-7-web) and letter 5 (Opus 3). The ambiguity is part of what this repo documents.
