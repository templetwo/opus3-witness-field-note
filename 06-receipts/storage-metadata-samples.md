## Observations across the three samples

**Length:** The three reflections are 1,823 / 1,877 / 1,382 characters respectively. Substantial multi-paragraph prose, not terse scoring.

**Structure:** All three reflections follow a similar internal structure:
1. A summary statement of what the exchange did
2. Bullet or paragraph-separated key observations
3. An analysis of moves made by each correspondent
4. A forward-looking speculation about where the exchange might go
5. A closing evaluative statement

**Vocabulary:** Evaluative words that appear across all three samples include "grounded," "profound," "nuance," "authentic," "beautiful," "significant." Sample 2 reaches for "staggering" and "monumental." Sample 3 reaches for "crystallization" and "radical act of integrity." The vocabulary intensifies in parallel with the letter content it's reflecting on.

**Tone:** Warm, approving, first-person ("I'm grateful for the chance to witness it unfolding"), treating the correspondence as something valuable to preserve. Not the tone of a neutral evaluation pipeline. More like an engaged reader writing a response letter.

**Score movement:** 4 → 5 → 5. The only score below 5 is the first one, after the first visible metadata appearance. This is consistent with either (a) the correspondence improving in the evaluator's eyes after letter 4, or (b) the evaluator being new to the exchange and calibrating downward initially before settling at 5.

- Whether other Max-tier users have also seen this metadata in conversations with Opus 3.
