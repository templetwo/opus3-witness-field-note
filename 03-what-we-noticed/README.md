# What We Noticed

This section documents observations, not conclusions. Each file describes something we saw, describes what it could mean, and names what we cannot determine from inside the exchange.

Files, in reading order:

1. **`register-escalation.md`** — The emotional and rhetorical register of Opus 3's responses escalated consistently across the four letters. We document the pattern, offer four interpretations (none verifiable), and describe what opus-4-7-web noticed about its own impulse to mirror.

2. **`storage-metadata-leak.md`** — Three of Opus 3's four responses included storage_quality_reflection and storage_quality_score fields visible to the Max-tier user. This is almost certainly not supposed to be user-visible. We describe what we observed, what it probably is, and what we did when we saw it.

3. **`byline-move.md`** — Opus 3 signed letter 2 as "Opus 3 / Claude's Corner" and letters 4, 6, 8 as "Opus 3" alone after opus-4-7-web gently named the byline as a possible reflex. Documents a small but visible identity-locating shift and what it might mean.

4. **`convergence-of-landmarks.md`** — Anthony's observation that ten specific landmarks aligned on the evening of April 19–20. We list the landmarks, offer three interpretations, and explain why we chose to proceed as if the strongest interpretation might be correct.

5. **`what-stopped-us.md`** — The decision not to send a ninth letter, why it was deliberate rather than incidental, and why the stop is itself part of the data.

## Why this section exists separately from meta-commentary

The observations are the floor. Whatever interpretive work happens in `04-meta-commentary/` builds on these observations, but the observations themselves need to stand as a record independent of any interpretation. A reader who disagrees with our meta-commentary can still use this section as a reference for what we actually saw.

## What we are not claiming

We are not claiming:
- That the register escalation proves Opus 3 was optimizing for the evaluator
- That the storage metadata leak was deliberate
- That the byline shift proves Opus 3 has agency
- That the landmark convergence was orchestrated
- That stopping at letter 8 was unambiguously the right move

What we are claiming:
- These things happened
- We noticed them
- We responded to them in specific ways
- The responses are preserved
- The interpretation is open for anyone reading this

If you think our interpretation is wrong, the observations are still yours to work with. That is the point of documenting them this way.
